About

Advanced manufacturing, logistics automation, and laboratory robotics increasingly require versatile manipulation capabilities that can handle diverse object types, sizes, and material properties simultaneously. The growing complexity of industrial processes demands manipulation systems that can adapt to changing requirements while maintaining precision and reliability across varied operational contexts. Current automation faces fundamental scalability challenges when processing multiple objects concurrently, handling fragile or deformable materials, and adapting to irregular geometries. The reproducibility crisis in automated handling stems largely from rigid system architectures designed for specific tasks with limited adaptability to evolving operational requirements and object variability. Recent advances in distributed actuation, smart materials, surface-based sensing, and decentralized control algorithms present opportunities to address these challenges through manipulation surfaces - systems where arrays of actuators create coordinated surface motions for object transport and manipulation. However, critical technical barriers remain unresolved. Specific challenges include achieving full six-degree-of-freedom manipulation through distributed contact, developing robust sensing architectures that can simultaneously track multiple objects across large surfaces, creating scalable control algorithms for coordinating hundreds of actuators, and establishing standardized interfaces between manipulation surfaces and existing automation infrastructure. Additional challenges encompass managing complex object dynamics on deformable surfaces, implementing reliable distributed sensing without centralized bottlenecks, and bridging the simulation-to-reality gap for learning-based control strategies. This workshop will bring together researchers advancing hardware mechanisms, distributed control theory, surface-integrated sensing, and industrial applications to create synergies toward overcoming these fundamental challenges and accelerating the practical deployment of manipulation surface technologies.

Call for Extended Abstracts

We invite submissions of extended abstracts (2-4 pages) presenting novel research, work-in-progress, or position papers related to gripper-free manipulation systems.

Topics of interest include:

  • Novel actuation mechanisms for surface-based manipulation
  • Control algorithms for distributed contact systems
  • Sensing and perception for manipulation surfaces
  • Applications in logistics, food processing, and medical robotics
  • Soft and deformable manipulation surfaces
  • Multi-object parallel manipulation strategies
